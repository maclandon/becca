Common questions

Everything you need
to know before booking

Can you actually diagnose my condition?
+

I can review your symptoms, history, and existing records and give you a professional clinical opinion on what's likely going on — but a formal diagnosis requires an in-person comprehensive exam. What I can tell you is whether your situation is urgent, what it sounds like, and what kind of specialist or test would confirm it.

Do you accept insurance?
+

No — consultations are a flat cash fee paid at booking. This keeps things simple and means no surprise bills. HSA and FSA cards are accepted. These sessions are not billed through insurance, which also means there's no claim, no referral requirement, and no waitlist.

I'm not in NY or PA. Can I still book?
+

Unfortunately, no. I'm licensed to practice in New York and Pennsylvania, and telehealth regulations require that I only see clients who are physically located in states where I hold a license. If you're outside those states, I'm not able to see you at this time.

Is this HIPAA compliant?
+

Yes. All video consultations are conducted through a HIPAA-compliant platform, and your intake information is handled securely. Your health information is never shared with third parties.

What if I need an in-person exam after?
+

That's often exactly the point. I'll tell you what kind of provider to see, what specific tests to ask for, and what questions to bring. You'll walk into that appointment knowing exactly what to ask — instead of starting from scratch with a stranger.

Can you write a glasses or contact lens prescription?
+

No. Writing a prescription requires in-person testing — specifically a refraction, which can't be performed remotely. If you need a new prescription, I can help you find the right in-person provider and explain what to expect from that visit.
